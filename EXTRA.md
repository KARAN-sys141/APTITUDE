# Complete Campus Placement Aptitude — Formulas & Shortcuts Guide

---

## 1. NUMBER SYSTEM

**Divisibility Rules**
- By 2: last digit even
- By 3: sum of digits divisible by 3
- By 4: last 2 digits divisible by 4
- By 8: last 3 digits divisible by 8
- By 9: sum of digits divisible by 9
- By 11: (sum of odd-position digits) − (sum of even-position digits) = 0 or multiple of 11

**LCM & HCF**
- LCM × HCF = Product of two numbers
- HCF of fractions = HCF of numerators / LCM of denominators
- LCM of fractions = LCM of numerators / HCF of denominators

**Shortcuts**
- Sum of first n natural numbers = n(n+1)/2
- Sum of first n odd numbers = n²
- Sum of first n even numbers = n(n+1)
- Sum of squares of first n naturals = n(n+1)(2n+1)/6
- Sum of cubes of first n naturals = [n(n+1)/2]²

---

## 2. PERCENTAGE

**Core Formulas**
- % = (Part / Whole) × 100
- New value after x% increase = Original × (1 + x/100)
- New value after x% decrease = Original × (1 − x/100)

**Successive % Change**
- Net change = a + b + ab/100 (when two % changes a and b happen)
- If a = b = 10% increase → net = 10 + 10 + 1 = 21%

**Key Fraction-% Conversions (memorize these)**
- 1/6 = 16.67%, 1/7 = 14.28%, 1/8 = 12.5%, 1/9 = 11.11%, 1/11 = 9.09%, 1/12 = 8.33%

**When to use:** Any question with "increase/decrease by %" or comparing values — use multiplier method, not pen-paper subtraction.

---

## 3. PROFIT & LOSS

**Formulas**
- Profit = SP − CP
- Loss = CP − SP
- Profit% = (Profit / CP) × 100
- Loss% = (Loss / CP) × 100
- SP = CP × (100 + P%) / 100
- CP = SP × 100 / (100 + P%)
- Discount = Marked Price − Selling Price
- Discount% = (Discount / MP) × 100

**Dishonest Seller (false weight)**
- Profit% = (True weight − False weight) / False weight × 100

**Two articles sold at same SP:**
- If one at x% profit and other at x% loss → always a loss = x²/100 %

**When to use:** SP/CP given → find profit%; MP/Discount given → find SP first.

---

## 4. SIMPLE & COMPOUND INTEREST

**Simple Interest**
- SI = (P × R × T) / 100
- Amount = P + SI

**Compound Interest**
- A = P × (1 + R/100)^T
- CI = A − P

**Compounded half-yearly:** A = P × (1 + R/200)^(2T)
**Compounded quarterly:** A = P × (1 + R/400)^(4T)

**CI − SI Shortcut (2 years):** CI − SI = P(R/100)²
**CI − SI Shortcut (3 years):** CI − SI = P(R/100)²(3 + R/100)

**When to use:** "Per annum compounded annually" → standard CI formula. If they say "half-yearly" → halve rate, double time.

---

## 5. RATIO & PROPORTION

**Formulas**
- If a:b = c:d → ad = bc (cross multiplication)
- Componendo: (a+b)/(a−b) = (c+d)/(c−d)
- Mean proportional of a and b = √(ab)
- Third proportional of a, b = b²/a
- Fourth proportional of a, b, c = bc/a

**Mixing (Alligation)**
- (Quantity of cheaper) / (Quantity of dearer) = (Dearer price − Mean price) / (Mean price − Cheaper price)

**When to use alligation:** Any mixture/blending or average rate question → draw the alligation cross directly.

---

## 6. TIME, SPEED & DISTANCE

**Core**
- Speed = Distance / Time
- Average speed (same distance) = 2S1×S2 / (S1+S2) — NOT arithmetic mean
- Average speed (same time) = (S1+S2)/2

**Relative Speed**
- Same direction: S1 − S2
- Opposite direction: S1 + S2

**Train Problems**
- Train crossing a pole/person: Time = Length of train / Speed
- Train crossing a platform: Time = (Length of train + Length of platform) / Speed
- Two trains crossing each other: Time = (L1 + L2) / Relative Speed

**Unit Conversion**
- km/h → m/s: multiply by 5/18
- m/s → km/h: multiply by 18/5

**Boat & Stream**
- Downstream speed = B + R (boat + river)
- Upstream speed = B − R
- B = (Downstream + Upstream) / 2
- R = (Downstream − Upstream) / 2

**When to use:** Always convert units first. Relative speed is the most commonly tested concept.

---

## 7. TIME & WORK

**Formulas**
- If A does work in n days → A's 1-day work = 1/n
- A and B together: 1/A + 1/B = 1/Total days
- Combined days = AB / (A+B)

**Work efficiency shortcut**
- A is twice as fast as B → A takes half the time of B
- If M men do W work in D days at H hours/day → MDH/W = constant (use to compare two scenarios)

**Pipes & Cisterns**
- Inlet fills: +1/n per hour
- Outlet drains: −1/n per hour
- Net work = sum of all

**When to use:** LCM method is fastest — take LCM of all days as total work, compute each person's per-day units, then divide.

---

## 8. AVERAGES

**Formulas**
- Average = Sum / Count
- New average when one element added: (Old sum + New element) / (n+1)
- If average of n numbers is A and one wrong number x is replaced by y: New average = A + (y−x)/n

**Weighted Average**
- Avg = (w1×a1 + w2×a2) / (w1+w2)

**When to use:** Average-based questions with replacement → find change in sum, divide by n. Don't recalculate everything.

---

## 9. ALGEBRA — EQUATIONS & INEQUALITIES

**Key Identities (must memorize)**
- (a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b²
- (a−b)² = a² − 2ab + b²
- a² − b² = (a+b)(a−b)
- (a+b)³ = a³ + 3a²b + 3ab² + b³
- a³ + b³ = (a+b)(a² − ab + b²)
- a³ − b³ = (a−b)(a² + ab + b²)
- If a + b + c = 0 → a³ + b³ + c³ = 3abc

**Quadratic**
- Sum of roots = −b/a
- Product of roots = c/a
- Discriminant = b² − 4ac → if >0: real roots, =0: equal roots, <0: no real roots

**When to use:** Don't expand blindly — look for (a+b)² or a²−b² pattern first.

---

## 10. GEOMETRY

**Areas**
- Triangle = ½ × base × height
- Triangle (Heron's) = √[s(s−a)(s−b)(s−c)] where s = (a+b+c)/2
- Equilateral triangle = (√3/4) × side²
- Rectangle = l × b
- Square = side²
- Circle = πr²
- Semicircle = πr²/2
- Trapezium = ½ × (sum of parallel sides) × height
- Rhombus = ½ × d1 × d2

**Volumes**
- Cube = a³, Surface area = 6a²
- Cuboid = lbh, SA = 2(lb+bh+lh)
- Cylinder = πr²h, Curved SA = 2πrh
- Cone = ⅓πr²h, Curved SA = πrl, l = √(r²+h²)
- Sphere = (4/3)πr³, SA = 4πr²
- Hemisphere = (2/3)πr³, Curved SA = 2πr²

**Pythagorean Triplets (memorize)**
- 3,4,5 | 5,12,13 | 8,15,17 | 7,24,25 | 9,40,41

**Angles**
- Sum of angles in triangle = 180°
- Exterior angle = sum of two non-adjacent interior angles
- Sum of interior angles of n-sided polygon = (n−2) × 180°

---

## 11. PERMUTATION & COMBINATION

**Formulas**
- nPr = n! / (n−r)!
- nCr = n! / [r!(n−r)!]
- nCr = nC(n−r)
- nC0 = nCn = 1

**Key Rules**
- Arrangement of n things with repetition = nⁿ
- Circular arrangement = (n−1)!
- If 2 things always together → treat as 1 unit, multiply by 2!
- If 2 things never together → Total − (both together)

**When to use:** "arrange/order" → permutation. "select/choose/group" → combination.

---

## 12. PROBABILITY

**Formulas**
- P(E) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes
- P(not E) = 1 − P(E)
- P(A and B) — independent: P(A) × P(B)
- P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A∩B)
- P(A|B) = P(A∩B) / P(B)

**Standard Sample Spaces**
- Coin: 2 outcomes, 2 coins: 4, n coins: 2ⁿ
- Die: 6 outcomes, 2 dice: 36
- Cards: 52 total, 4 suits × 13 cards each, 12 face cards

---

## 13. SERIES & SEQUENCES

**AP (Arithmetic Progression)**
- nth term: a + (n−1)d
- Sum of n terms: n/2 × [2a + (n−1)d] or n/2 × (first + last)

**GP (Geometric Progression)**
- nth term: a × rⁿ⁻¹
- Sum of n terms: a(rⁿ−1)/(r−1) for r≠1
- Sum of infinite GP (|r|<1): a/(1−r)

**When to use:** Identify if differences are constant (AP) or ratio is constant (GP) first.

---

## GENERAL TEST-TAKING STRATEGY

1. **Percentage & Ratio questions** — use fraction conversion + mental math, avoid long division
2. **Time-Speed-Distance** — draw a quick table: Speed | Time | Distance
3. **Work problems** — always go LCM method
4. **Mixture/Alligation** — draw the cross, never solve equations
5. **Profit-Loss with two articles at same SP** — remember it's always a loss formula directly
6. **Geometry** — memorize Pythagorean triplets, saves 30 seconds per question
7. **For 20-question daily tests** — first solve what you're confident in, mark uncertain ones, return later. Don't spend >90 seconds on any single question.

## 14. PROBLEMS ON AGES

**Setup trick:** Always assume present age as x, then form equation.

- If ratio of ages A:B = m:n → A = mk, B = nk
- n years ago → subtract n from present age
- n years hence → add n to present age
- "Ages are in ratio" after/before → form two equations and solve

**Common shortcut:** If A is twice as old as B and 10 years ago A was 3× older → make two equations, solve simultaneously. Don't overcomplicate.

## 15. CALENDAR

**Key Concepts**
- Ordinary year = 365 days = 52 weeks + 1 odd day
- Leap year = 366 days = 52 weeks + 2 odd days
- Century year is leap only if divisible by 400 (100, 200, 300 are NOT leap years)

**Odd days in months (memorize)**
- Jan=3, Feb=0(ordinary)/1(leap), Mar=3, Apr=2, May=3, Jun=2, Jul=3, Aug=3, Sep=2, Oct=3, Nov=2, Dec=3

**Day code mapping**
- 0=Sun, 1=Mon, 2=Tue, 3=Wed, 4=Thu, 5=Fri, 6=Sat

**Odd days in centuries**
- 100 years = 5 odd days
- 200 years = 3 odd days
- 300 years = 1 odd day
- 400 years = 0 odd days

**When to use:** "What day was Jan 1, 1901?" or "Same calendar as 2007 will repeat in which year?" — always use odd days method.

## 16. CLOCK

**Key Formulas**
- Minute hand moves 360° in 60 min → 6° per minute
- Hour hand moves 360° in 12 hours → 0.5° per minute
- Relative speed of minute hand over hour hand = 5.5° per minute
- Angle between hands at H hours M minutes = |30H − 5.5M|

**Minutes past H for hands to coincide:**
- At H o'clock, hands meet after = 60H/11 minutes

**For right angle (90°):** hands at 90° twice every hour — 22 times in 12 hours (not 24, because they overlap twice)

**For straight line (180°):** 11 times in 12 hours

**Clock gaining/losing:**
- If clock shows X min fast in Y hours → find error per hour and scale

## 17. DECIMAL FRACTIONS

**Key Rules**
- To convert recurring decimal x̄ to fraction: put repeated digits in numerator, same number of 9s in denominator
- 0.3̄ = 3/9 = 1/3
- 0.27̄ = 27/99 = 3/11
- Mixed recurring: 0.13̄ = (13−1)/90 = 12/90 = 2/15 → (all digits − non-recurring digits) / (9s for recurring × 0s for non-recurring)

**Operations**
- Multiplying decimals: ignore decimals, multiply, then place decimal point (total decimal places = sum of both)
- BODMAS always applies for simplification

## 18. SIMPLIFICATION

**BODMAS order:** Bracket → Of → Division → Multiplication → Addition → Subtraction

**Useful identities for quick calculation**
- a × (b+c) = ab + ac
- (a+b)(a−b) = a²−b²
- 999 × 999 = (1000−1)² = 1000000 − 2000 + 1 = 998001
- For large multiplications: use (a+b)² or (a−b)² form

**When to use:** Identify if any algebraic identity applies before doing raw multiplication.

## 19. SQUARE ROOT & CUBE ROOT

**Perfect squares to memorize (1–30):** 1,4,9,16,25,36,49,64,81,100,121,144,169,196,225,256,289,324,361,400,441,484,529,576,625,676,729,784,841,900

**Perfect cubes to memorize:** 1,8,27,64,125,216,343,512,729,1000

**Shortcut for √ of large numbers**
- Find nearest perfect square and estimate
- √(a²+r) ≈ a + r/2a (for small r)
- Example: √170 ≈ √169 + 1/(2×13) = 13.04

**Properties**
- √(a×b) = √a × √b
- √(a/b) = √a / √b
- Cube root of product = product of cube roots

## 20. SURDS & INDICES

**Index Laws**
- aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ
- aᵐ / aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ
- (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ
- (ab)ⁿ = aⁿbⁿ
- a⁰ = 1
- a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ
- a^(1/n) = ⁿ√a
- a^(m/n) = ⁿ√(aᵐ)

**Surd Rules**
- √a × √b = √(ab)
- √a / √b = √(a/b)
- Rationalization: multiply numerator & denominator by conjugate
- 1/(a+√b) → multiply by (a−√b)/(a−√b)

**When to use:** Any question with fractional powers or "√" in denominator → rationalize first.

---

## 21. CHAIN RULE (Direct & Inverse Proportion)

**Direct Proportion:** More A → More B (same direction)
**Inverse Proportion:** More A → Less B (opposite direction)

**Formula:**
M₁ × D₁ × H₁ / W₁ = M₂ × D₂ × H₂ / W₂

Where M=men, D=days, H=hours/day, W=work

**Rule:** Write all given values in a row, then for each variable: if direct proportion → write as big/small such that answer increases in same direction; if inverse → flip it.

**When to use:** Any question with multiple changing variables like "X men finish Y work in Z days, how many men for W work in V days?"

---

## 22. LOGARITHM

**Basic Rules**
- log_a(1) = 0
- log_a(a) = 1
- log_a(xy) = log_a(x) + log_a(y)
- log_a(x/y) = log_a(x) − log_a(y)
- log_a(xⁿ) = n × log_a(x)
- log_a(x) = log_b(x) / log_b(a) (change of base)
- log_a(x) = 1 / log_x(a)

**Standard values**
- log 2 = 0.3010, log 3 = 0.4771, log 5 = 0.6990, log 7 = 0.8451
- ln(e) = 1, log(10) = 1

**When to use:** Express all terms in same base first, then apply rules.

## 23. PARTNERSHIP

**Formulas**
- If capital ratio = A:B → profit ratio = A:B (for same time)
- If time is different: Ratio of profit = Capital × Time for each partner
- P₁:P₂ = C₁×T₁ : C₂×T₂

**Working partner** gets extra salary first from total profit, then remaining is divided in ratio.

**When to use:** Always convert to Capital × Time product before finding ratio.

## 24. RACES & GAMES

**Key Terms**
- "A gives B a start of X metres" → B starts X m ahead of A
- "A gives B a start of X seconds" → B starts X sec before A
- "A beats B by X metres in a race of Y metres" → when A finishes Y, B is at (Y−X)
- "A beats B by X seconds" → A finishes X seconds before B

**Speed Ratio**
- If A beats B by d metres in a race of L metres → Speed of A / Speed of B = L / (L−d)

**Dead heat:** Both reach finish at same time.

## 25. HEIGHT & DISTANCE (Trigonometry)

**Angles of Elevation & Depression**
- Angle of elevation: looking UP at object from ground
- Angle of depression: looking DOWN at object from height

**Standard Values (must memorize)**

| Angle | sin | cos | tan |
|-------|-----|-----|-----|
| 0° | 0 | 1 | 0 |
| 30° | 1/2 | √3/2 | 1/√3 |
| 45° | 1/√2 | 1/√2 | 1 |
| 60° | √3/2 | 1/2 | √3 |
| 90° | 1 | 0 | ∞ |

**Key Relations**
- tan θ = Height / Base (opposite/adjacent) — used in 90% of H&D questions
- sin θ = Height / Hypotenuse
- cos θ = Base / Hypotenuse

**Common scenarios**
- Tower height with angle of elevation at distance d: Height = d × tan θ
- Two poles, string between: use Pythagoras on the right triangle
- Same object from two angles: H = (d × tan α × tan β) / (tan α − tan β) where d is distance between two observation points

## 26. STOCKS & SHARES

**Key Terms**
- Face Value (FV): printed value of share (usually ₹100)
- Market Value (MV): current price in market
- Dividend: % paid on Face Value
- Income = (Dividend% × FV)
- Return% = (Income / MV) × 100

**Formulas**
- Investment = Number of shares × MV
- Annual income = Number of shares × (Dividend% × FV / 100)
- To get ₹X income → Number of shares = X / (Dividend% × FV / 100)

**When to use:** Always calculate income on Face Value, not Market Value. Return is on Market Value.

---

## 27. TRUE DISCOUNT

**Key Terms**
- Present Worth (PW): actual value now
- True Discount (TD): PW × R × T / 100
- Amount (A) = PW + TD (future amount)

**Formulas**
- PW = 100 × A / (100 + R×T)
- TD = A × R×T / (100 + R×T)
- TD = SI on PW (not on Amount)
- BD − TD = SI on TD

---

## 28. BANKER'S DISCOUNT

**Key Terms**
- Banker's Discount (BD): SI on face value for unexpired time
- Banker's Gain (BG) = BD − TD
- BG = TD²/PW

**Formulas**
- BD = Amount × R × T / 100
- TD = BD × 100 / (100 + R×T)
- BD/TD = A/PW

**When to use:** BD is always > TD for same bill. BG is the extra a banker earns.

---

## 29. ODD MAN OUT & SERIES

**Types of Series**
- Arithmetic: constant difference → find next term with common difference
- Geometric: constant ratio → find next term with ratio
- Perfect squares: 1,4,9,16,25... → look for n²
- Perfect cubes: 1,8,27,64... → look for n³
- Fibonacci: each = sum of previous two
- Mixed: alternating two series interleaved
- Prime numbers: 2,3,5,7,11,13...

**Finding Odd Man Out**
- Find the pattern, identify which number breaks it
- Check: differences, ratios, squares, cubes, primes
- If all even but one odd → that's the odd one
- If all prime but one composite → that's the odd one

**When to use:** First write differences between consecutive terms. If differences are constant/in AP/GP, you know the type immediately.

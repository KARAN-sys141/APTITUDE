# TOPIC 6: RATIO & PROPORTION — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic Ratio Simplification

**Q.** Simplify 48:72:96
**Ans:** Divide by HCF=24 → **2:3:4**

**Q.** A:B = 5:7, B:C = 14:9. Find A:C.
**Ans:** Make B common. B=7 and B=14 → multiply first by 2 → A:B=10:14, B:C=14:9
A:C = **10:9**

---

## Variety 2 — Ratio to Actual Values

**Q.** A:B:C = 3:5:7 and total = 450. Find each.
**Ans:** Total parts = 15
A = 450×3/15 = **90**, B = **150**, C = **210**

---

## Variety 3 — Making A:B:C from Two Ratios

**Q.** A:B = 3:4, B:C = 6:7. Find A:B:C.
**Ans:** B=4 and B=6 → LCM=12 → multiply first by 3, second by 2
A:B = 9:12, B:C = 12:14 → A:B:C = **9:12:14**

**Q.** A:B = 2:3, B:C = 5:7, C:D = 3:4. Find A:D.
**Ans:** A:B=2:3 ×5 → 10:15
B:C=5:7 ×3 → 15:21
C:D=3:4 ×7 → 21:28
A:D = **10:28 = 5:14**

---

## Variety 4 — Compounded Ratios

**Q.** Find compounded ratio of 2:3, 4:5, 6:7.
**Ans:** (2×4×6):(3×5×7) = 48:105 = **16:35**

**Q.** Duplicate ratio of 3:4?
**Ans:** 3²:4² = **9:16**

**Q.** Sub-duplicate ratio of 25:49?
**Ans:** √25:√49 = **5:7**

---

## Variety 5 — Proportion (Fourth Proportional)

**Q.** Fourth proportional to 3, 5, 12?
**Ans:** 3:5 = 12:x → x = 5×12/3 = **20**

**Q.** Mean proportional of 4 and 25?
**Ans:** √(4×25) = √100 = **10**

**Q.** Third proportional to 6 and 18?
**Ans:** 6:18 = 18:x → x = 18²/6 = **54**

---

## Variety 6 — Ratio Comparison

**Q.** Which is greater: 5:8 or 7:11?
**Ans:** 5/8 = 0.625, 7/11 = 0.636 → **7:11 is greater**

**Q.** Arrange in ascending: 3:5, 7:10, 13:20
**Ans:** 0.60, 0.70, 0.65 → **3:5 < 13:20 < 7:10**

---

## Variety 7 — Mixture (Ratio Changes by Adding)

**Q.** Milk:water = 4:1 in 20L vessel. How much water to add to make 2:3?
**Ans:** Milk = 16L, Water = 4L
New ratio milk:water = 2:3 → 16/(4+x) = 2/3
48 = 8+2x → 2x = 40 → x = **20L**

**Q.** Milk:water = 5:3. 16L removed, replaced with water. New ratio = 3:5. Find original quantity.
**Ans:** Let total = V. After removing 16L: milk = 5V/8 − 10, water = 3V/8 − 6 + 16
(5V/8 − 10)/(3V/8 + 10) = 3/5
5(5V/8−10) = 3(3V/8+10)
25V/8 − 50 = 9V/8 + 30
16V/8 = 80 → 2V = 80 → V = **40L**

---

## Variety 8 — Partnership (Ratio-Based Profit)

**Q.** A:B invest ₹4000:₹6000 for same time. Total profit ₹5000. B's share?
**Ans:** Ratio = 4:6 = 2:3. B = 5000×3/5 = **₹3000**

**Q.** A invests ₹5000 for 8 months, B invests ₹6000 for 6 months. Profit ₹5480. A's share?
**Ans:** A = 5000×8 = 40000, B = 6000×6 = 36000
Ratio = 40:36 = 10:9
A's share = 5480×10/19 = **₹2884.21**

---

## Variety 9 — Ratio in Ages

**Q.** Present ratio A:B = 3:5. After 8 years ratio = 5:7. Present ages?
**Ans:** A=3k, B=5k
(3k+8)/(5k+8) = 5/7
21k+56 = 25k+40 → 4k=16 → k=4
A = **12**, B = **20**

---

## Variety 10 — Direct & Inverse Proportion

**Q.** 8 workers earn ₹4800 in 6 days. 10 workers earn in 4 days?
**Ans:** Workers and earnings → direct. Days and earnings → direct.
Earnings = 4800 × (10/8) × (4/6) = 4800 × 5/4 × 2/3 = **₹4000**

**Q.** 6 pipes fill tank in 8 hours. 4 pipes fill in?
**Ans:** Inverse proportion. 6×8 = 4×T → T = **12 hours**

---

## Variety 11 — Alligation (Mixture of Two Prices)

**Q.** Tea at ₹40/kg and ₹60/kg mixed to get ₹50/kg. Ratio?
**Ans:** Alligation cross:
Cheaper diff = 50−40 = 10, Dearer diff = 60−50 = 10
Ratio = **1:1**

**Q.** Milk at ₹16/L mixed with water (₹0) to sell at ₹10/L. Ratio milk:water?
**Ans:** Cheaper(water) diff = 10−0=10, Dearer(milk) diff = 16−10=6
Ratio water:milk = 6:10 = 3:5
So milk:water = **5:3**

---

## Variety 12 — Time-Based Ratio (Working Partner)

**Q.** A invested ₹8000. B joined after 4 months with ₹12000. Profit after 1 year ₹7300. B's share?
**Ans:** A = 8000×12 = 96000, B = 12000×8 = 96000
Ratio = 1:1 → B's share = **₹3650**

# TOPIC 7: NUMBER SYSTEM & REMAINDERS — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Divisibility Rules

**Q.** Is 876543 divisible by 9?
**Ans:** Sum = 8+7+6+5+4+3 = 33. Not divisible by 9. **No**

**Q.** Find smallest number to add to 1234 to make it divisible by 9.
**Ans:** Sum of digits = 10. Need sum = 18. Add **8**. (1242 → sum=9? 1+2+4+2=9 ✓)

---

## Variety 2 — LCM & HCF Applications

**Q.** HCF of 72 and 120? LCM?
**Ans:** 72 = 2³×3², 120 = 2³×3×5
HCF = 2³×3 = **24**
LCM = 2³×3²×5 = **360**
Verify: 72×120 = 24×360 = 8640 ✓

**Q.** Three bells ring at 12, 15, 20 min intervals. All ring together at 6am. Next together?
**Ans:** LCM(12,15,20) = 60 min → **7:00 am**

---

## Variety 3 — Remainder Problems (Basic)

**Q.** What is remainder when 385 is divided by 8?
**Ans:** 385 = 48×8 + 1 → Remainder = **1**

**Q.** When N divided by 6 remainder is 4. Remainder when 3N divided by 6?
**Ans:** N = 6k+4 → 3N = 18k+12 = 18k+12. 12/6 = 2 remainder 0 → **Remainder = 0**

---

## Variety 4 — Remainder of Powers (Cyclicity)

**Q.** Remainder when 2^74 is divided by 3?
**Ans:** 2¹ mod 3=2, 2²=1, 2³=2, 2⁴=1 → cycle of 2
74 is even → remainder = **1**

**Q.** Remainder when 7^103 is divided by 5?
**Ans:** 7 mod 5=2. Powers of 2 mod 5: 2,4,3,1,2... cycle=4
103 = 4×25+3 → same as 2³=8 mod 5 = **3**

**Q.** Last digit of 7^100?
**Ans:** Last digits of 7: 7,9,3,1,7... cycle=4. 100÷4=25 rem 0 → same as 7⁴ → last digit = **1**

---

## Variety 5 — Last Two Digits

**Q.** Last two digits of 3^100?
**Ans:** Pattern of last two digits of 3: cycle=20
100÷20=5 rem 0 → same as 3^20 → last two digits = **01**

**Q.** Last two digits of 7^100?
**Ans:** Cycle=4: 07,49,43,01. 100÷4=25 rem 0 → **01**

---

## Variety 6 — Number of Digits

**Q.** How many digits in 2^50? (log2=0.3010)
**Ans:** log(2^50) = 50×0.3010 = 15.05
Digits = ⌊15.05⌋+1 = **16**

**Q.** How many digits in 3^25? (log3=0.4771)
**Ans:** 25×0.4771 = 11.9275 → ⌊11.9275⌋+1 = **12**

---

## Variety 7 — Sum Formulas

**Q.** Sum of first 50 natural numbers?
**Ans:** n(n+1)/2 = 50×51/2 = **1275**

**Q.** Sum of squares of first 10 natural numbers?
**Ans:** n(n+1)(2n+1)/6 = 10×11×21/6 = **385**

**Q.** Sum of cubes of first 5 natural numbers?
**Ans:** [n(n+1)/2]² = [15]² = **225**

---

## Variety 8 — Consecutive Numbers

**Q.** Product of two consecutive integers is 552. Find them.
**Ans:** n(n+1)=552. √552≈23.5 → try 23×24=552 ✓ → **23 and 24**

**Q.** Sum of three consecutive odd numbers is 81. Find them.
**Ans:** (n-2)+n+(n+2)=81 → 3n=81 → n=27 → **25, 27, 29**

---

## Variety 9 — Divisors & Factors

**Q.** How many factors does 360 have?
**Ans:** 360 = 2³×3²×5¹
Factors = (3+1)(2+1)(1+1) = 4×3×2 = **24**

**Q.** How many even factors does 120 have?
**Ans:** 120 = 2³×3×5. Even factors must have at least one 2.
Total factors = 4×2×2=16. Odd factors (no 2) = 1×2×2=4.
Even = 16−4 = **12**

---

## Variety 10 — Finding Unknown Digit

**Q.** In 7X4 divisible by 8, find X.
**Ans:** Last 3 digits 7X4 divisible by 8. Try X=0: 704/8=88 ✓ → **X=0**
Also X=8: 784/8=98 ✓ → **X=0 or X=8**

**Q.** 4X5Y divisible by both 4 and 9. Find X and Y.
**Ans:** Div by 4 → last two digits 5Y div by 4 → Y=2 (52/4=13) or Y=6 (56/4=14)
Div by 9 → 4+X+5+Y = multiple of 9 → 9+X+Y = 9 or 18
If Y=2: X=−2 (invalid) or X=7 (9+7+2=18 ✓) → **X=7, Y=2**

---

## Variety 11 — Remainder Theorem Shortcut

**Q.** Remainder when (17^3 + 5) divided by 6?
**Ans:** 17 mod 6 = 5. 5³=125. 125 mod 6: 125=20×6+5 → 5. Then 5+5=10. 10 mod 6 = **4**

**Q.** Remainder when 100! divided by 103?
**Ans:** 103 is prime. By Wilson's theorem variant — 100! mod 103 = 100!/103... this needs advanced approach. In placement: any n! where n < prime p → remainder of n!/p = 0 only if p≤n. Since 103>100, remainder ≠ 0. This variety rarely appears — skip for now.

# TOPIC 8: AVERAGES — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic Average

**Q.** Average of 12, 18, 24, 30, 36?
**Ans:** Sum = 120. Average = 120/5 = **24**

**Q.** Average of first 20 multiples of 7?
**Ans:** Multiples: 7,14...140. Average = (first+last)/2 = (7+140)/2 = **73.5**

---

## Variety 2 — Adding / Removing an Element

**Q.** Average of 25 numbers = 40. One number 60 is removed. New average?
**Ans:** New sum = 25×40 − 60 = 940. New average = 940/24 = **39.17**

**Q.** Average of 10 numbers = 15. One more number added, average becomes 16. New number?
**Ans:** Old sum = 150. New sum = 16×11 = 176. New number = **26**

---

## Variety 3 — Replacement

**Q.** Average of 20 numbers = 36. If 45 replaced by 25, new average?
**Ans:** Sum change = 25−45 = −20. New sum = 720−20 = 700. Average = 700/20 = **35**

**Q.** Average of class = 40. If teacher (age 60) included, average becomes 41. Class size?
**Ans:** New sum − old sum = 60. (n+1)×41 − n×40 = 60
41n+41−40n = 60 → n+41 = 60 → n = **19**

---

## Variety 4 — Weighted Average

**Q.** Class A has 30 students avg 50, Class B has 20 students avg 40. Combined average?
**Ans:** (30×50 + 20×40)/(30+20) = (1500+800)/50 = 2300/50 = **46**

**Q.** Man travels 3 hours at 60 km/h and 5 hours at 80 km/h. Average speed?
**Ans:** Total distance = 180+400=580. Total time = 8. Average = 580/8 = **72.5 km/h**
(Same time → can't use harmonic mean. Use actual distances.)

---

## Variety 5 — Average with Consecutive Numbers

**Q.** Average of 5 consecutive even numbers is 34. Largest?
**Ans:** Middle number = 34. Numbers: 30,32,34,36,38. Largest = **38**

**Q.** Average of 7 consecutive numbers is 20. Sum?
**Ans:** Middle = 20. Numbers 17 to 23. Sum = 7×20 = **140**

---

## Variety 6 — Wrong Number Corrected

**Q.** Average of 30 numbers = 50. Later found one number read as 45 instead of 54. Correct average?
**Ans:** Correct sum = 30×50 − 45 + 54 = 1500+9 = 1509
Correct average = 1509/30 = **50.3**

---

## Variety 7 — Average Speed Trap

**Q.** Goes from A to B at 40 km/h, returns at 60 km/h. Average speed?
**Ans:** = 2×40×60/(40+60) = 4800/100 = **48 km/h** (NOT 50)

---

## Variety 8 — Finding Missing Number

**Q.** Average of 6 numbers = 30. Five numbers are 25, 28, 32, 35, 40. Sixth?
**Ans:** Sum = 180. Known sum = 160. Sixth = **20**

---

## Variety 9 — Average of First N Naturals / Odd / Even

**Q.** Average of first n natural numbers?
**Ans:** (n+1)/2

**Q.** Average of first 100 natural numbers?
**Ans:** 101/2 = **50.5**

**Q.** Average of first n odd numbers?
**Ans:** = n (always)
First 10 odd numbers average = **10**

**Q.** Average of first n even numbers?
**Ans:** = n+1
First 10 even numbers average = **11**

---

## Variety 10 — Exam Marks Combination

**Q.** Student scores avg 75 in 6 subjects. Drops one subject with 60 marks. New average?
**Ans:** Sum = 450. Remove 60 → 390. Average = 390/5 = **78**

**Q.** Average marks of boys = 65, girls = 75. Overall = 70. Ratio of boys to girls?
**Ans:** Alligation: Boys diff = 70−65=5, Girls diff=75−70=5. Ratio = **1:1**

# TOPIC 9: PROBABILITY — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic Probability

**Q.** A die rolled. P(prime number)?
**Ans:** Primes on die: 2,3,5 → 3 outcomes. P = 3/6 = **1/2**

**Q.** A number chosen from 1-20. P(divisible by 3 or 5)?
**Ans:** Div by 3: 3,6,9,12,15,18 = 6. Div by 5: 5,10,15,20 = 4. Both: 15 = 1.
P = (6+4−1)/20 = 9/20 = **0.45**

---

## Variety 2 — Complementary Probability

**Q.** P(at least one head) in 4 coin tosses?
**Ans:** P(none) = (1/2)⁴ = 1/16. P(at least one) = 1−1/16 = **15/16**

**Q.** P(at least one 6) in rolling two dice?
**Ans:** P(no 6) = (5/6)² = 25/36. P(at least one) = 1−25/36 = **11/36**

---

## Variety 3 — Two Dice Problems

**Q.** Two dice thrown. P(sum = 8)?
**Ans:** Pairs: (2,6),(3,5),(4,4),(5,3),(6,2) = 5 outcomes. P = 5/36

**Q.** P(sum > 10)?
**Ans:** Sum 11: (5,6),(6,5)=2. Sum 12: (6,6)=1. Total=3. P = 3/36 = **1/12**

**Q.** P(both same number)?
**Ans:** (1,1),(2,2)...(6,6) = 6. P = 6/36 = **1/6**

---

## Variety 4 — Card Problems

**Q.** One card drawn. P(red king)?
**Ans:** Red kings = 2 (hearts+diamonds). P = 2/52 = **1/26**

**Q.** P(face card or spade)?
**Ans:** Face cards = 12. Spades = 13. Face card spades = 3.
P = (12+13−3)/52 = 22/52 = **11/26**

**Q.** Two cards drawn. P(both aces)?
**Ans:** C(4,2)/C(52,2) = 6/1326 = **1/221**

---

## Variety 5 — Balls/Urn Without Replacement

**Q.** Bag: 5 red, 3 blue. Two drawn without replacement. P(both red)?
**Ans:** (5/8) × (4/7) = 20/56 = **5/14**

**Q.** P(first red, second blue)?
**Ans:** (5/8) × (3/7) = 15/56

**Q.** P(different colors)?
**Ans:** P(RB) + P(BR) = 15/56 + 15/56 = **30/56 = 15/28**

---

## Variety 6 — With Replacement

**Q.** Same bag. Two drawn WITH replacement. P(both red)?
**Ans:** (5/8) × (5/8) = **25/64**

**Trap:** With replacement → denominator stays same. Without → denominator decreases.

---

## Variety 7 — Independent Events

**Q.** P(A) = 1/3, P(B) = 1/4. A and B independent. P(both)?
**Ans:** 1/3 × 1/4 = **1/12**

**Q.** P(at least one occurs)?
**Ans:** 1 − P(neither) = 1 − (2/3)(3/4) = 1 − 1/2 = **1/2**

---

## Variety 8 — Conditional Probability

**Q.** Bag: 4 red, 6 blue. Two drawn without replacement. P(second red | first red)?
**Ans:** After one red removed: 3 red, 6 blue, total 9. P = 3/9 = **1/3**

**Q.** P(A∩B)=1/6, P(B)=1/3. Find P(A|B).
**Ans:** P(A|B) = (1/6)/(1/3) = **1/2**

---

## Variety 9 — Mutually Exclusive Events

**Q.** P(A)=0.4, P(B)=0.3, A and B mutually exclusive. P(A or B)?
**Ans:** P(A∪B) = 0.4+0.3 = **0.7**

**Q.** P(A)=0.4, P(B)=0.3, P(A∩B)=0.1. P(A or B)?
**Ans:** P(A∪B) = 0.4+0.3−0.1 = **0.6**

---

## Variety 10 — Coin Problems

**Q.** 3 coins tossed. P(exactly 2 heads)?
**Ans:** C(3,2) × (1/2)³ = 3/8

**Q.** P(at least 2 heads)?
**Ans:** P(2H) + P(3H) = 3/8 + 1/8 = **1/2**

---

## Variety 11 — Bayes' Theorem (Basic)

**Q.** Box 1 has 3 red 2 blue. Box 2 has 1 red 4 blue. Box chosen randomly, ball drawn is red. P(from Box 1)?
**Ans:** P(B1)=1/2, P(red|B1)=3/5, P(B2)=1/2, P(red|B2)=1/5
P(B1|red) = (1/2 × 3/5) / (1/2 × 3/5 + 1/2 × 1/5)
= (3/10) / (4/10) = **3/4**

# TOPIC 10: PERMUTATION & COMBINATION — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic Counting Principle

**Q.** 3 shirts, 4 pants, 2 shoes. Outfits possible?
**Ans:** 3×4×2 = **24**

**Q.** How many 2-digit numbers from {1,2,3,4,5} without repetition?
**Ans:** 5×4 = **20**

---

## Variety 2 — Permutation (Order Matters)

**Q.** In how many ways can 6 people stand in a row?
**Ans:** 6! = **720**

**Q.** In how many ways can 4 be chosen from 8 and arranged?
**Ans:** 8P4 = 8!/(8−4)! = 8×7×6×5 = **1680**

---

## Variety 3 — Combination (Order Doesn't Matter)

**Q.** Choose 3 from 8 people. Ways?
**Ans:** 8C3 = 8!/(3!×5!) = 56

**Q.** Committee of 5 from 10. Ways?
**Ans:** 10C5 = **252**

---

## Variety 4 — Arrangements with Repetition

**Q.** Arrangements of letters in "MISSISSIPPI"?
**Ans:** Total = 11 letters. M=1, I=4, S=4, P=2
= 11!/(4!×4!×2!) = 39916800/(24×24×2) = **34650**

**Q.** Arrangements of "MATHEMATICS"?
**Ans:** 11 letters. M=2, A=2, T=2, others×1
= 11!/(2!×2!×2!) = 39916800/8 = **4989600**

---

## Variety 5 — Conditional Arrangements (Together)

**Q.** 5 people in a row. A and B must be together. Ways?
**Ans:** Treat AB as 1 unit → 4! arrangements × 2! internal = 24×2 = **48**

**Q.** Letters of "ALGORITHM". Vowels always together.
**Ans:** Vowels: A,I,O,I = wait, ALGORITHM: A,L,G,O,R,I,T,H,M → vowels A,O,I = 3
Block + 6 consonants = 7 units → 7! × 3! = 5040×6 = **30240**

---

## Variety 6 — Conditional Arrangements (Never Together)

**Q.** 6 people in a row. A and B never together. Ways?
**Ans:** Total = 6! = 720. Together = 5!×2! = 240.
Never together = 720−240 = **480**

---

## Variety 7 — Circular Arrangement

**Q.** 8 people sit around a circular table. Ways?
**Ans:** (8−1)! = 7! = **5040**

**Q.** 5 men, 5 women in circle. All women together. Ways?
**Ans:** Treat 5 women as 1 block → 6 units in circle = (6−1)! = 120
Women internal = 5! = 120
Total = 120×120 = **14400**

---

## Variety 8 — Selection with Conditions

**Q.** Committee of 4 from 5 men and 4 women. At least 2 women. Ways?
**Ans:** Exactly 2W: C(4,2)×C(5,2) = 6×10 = 60
Exactly 3W: C(4,3)×C(5,1) = 4×5 = 20
Exactly 4W: C(4,4)×C(5,0) = 1×1 = 1
Total = **81**

**Q.** At least 1 man in committee of 4 from 5 men 4 women.
**Ans:** Total − all women = C(9,4) − C(4,4) = 126−1 = **125**

---

## Variety 9 — Number Formation with Digits

**Q.** 4-digit numbers from {1,2,3,4,5} without repetition divisible by 5.
**Ans:** Last digit must be 5. Fix 5 at units place. Remaining 3 places from 4 digits = 4×3×2 = 24 ways = **24**

**Q.** How many 4-digit even numbers from {0,1,2,3,4,5} without repetition?
**Ans:** Last digit even: 0, 2, 4

Case 1 (last digit = 0): First 3 places = 5×4×3 = 60
Case 2 (last digit = 2 or 4): First place ≠ 0 → 4 choices. Second = 4, Third = 3.
Each: 4×4×3 = 48. Two cases: 96.
Total = 60+96 = **156**

---

## Variety 10 — Handshakes / Matches (Combination Application)

**Q.** 10 people in a room. Everyone shakes hands with everyone else. Total handshakes?
**Ans:** C(10,2) = **45**

**Q.** In a round-robin tournament with 8 teams, total matches?
**Ans:** C(8,2) = **28**

---

## Variety 11 — At Least One (Complementary in P&C)

**Q.** From 4 Math and 5 Science books, select 4. At least one Math. Ways?
**Ans:** Total = C(9,4) = 126. None from Math = C(5,4) = 5.
At least one Math = 126−5 = **121**

**Q.** From 3 vowels and 5 consonants, 4-letter words with at least 2 vowels.
**Ans:** Exactly 2V: C(3,2)×C(5,2) = 3×10 = 30 → arrangements = 30×4! = 720
Exactly 3V: C(3,3)×C(5,1) = 1×5 = 5 → arrangements = 5×4! = 120
Total = **840**

---

## Variety 12 — Rank of a Word in Dictionary

**Q.** Find rank of word "RANK" in dictionary.
**Ans:** Letters in order: A, K, N, R
Words before R at first position: 3! = 6 (starting with A, K, N each)
Starting RA: letters left K,N → words before RANK: AN → 1 word (RAN_)
RAN: letters left K → only RANK
Starting RAK: 1 word (RAKN) before RANK? No — RAKN comes before RANK? R-A-K vs R-A-N → K<N so RAKN before RANK → 1 word

Rank = 6 + 1 + 1 + 1 = **9th**

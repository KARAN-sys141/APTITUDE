# TOPIC 1: PERCENTAGE — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic Percentage Calculation

**Q.** What is 35% of 480?
**Ans:** 480 × 35/100 = **168**

**Q.** 252 is what % of 360?
**Ans:** 252/360 × 100 = **70%**

---

## Variety 2 — Percentage Increase / Decrease

**Q.** A number increased from 240 to 288. % increase?
**Ans:** (288−240)/240 × 100 = 48/240 × 100 = **20%**

**Q.** Price decreased from 500 to 425. % decrease?
**Ans:** 75/500 × 100 = **15%**

---

## Variety 3 — Successive % Changes

**Q.** A number first increased by 20% then decreased by 15%. Net change?
**Ans:** Net = 20 + (−15) + (20×−15)/100 = 5 − 3 = **+2% increase**

**Q.** Price increased 10% then again 10%. Net?
**Ans:** 10 + 10 + 1 = **21% increase**

---

## Variety 4 — Reverse Percentage (Find Original)

**Q.** After 20% increase, value is 660. Original?
**Ans:** Original = 660 × 100/120 = **550**

**Q.** After 15% decrease, value is 425. Original?
**Ans:** Original = 425 × 100/85 = **500**

**Trap:** Never subtract 15% from 425 directly.

---

## Variety 5 — Consumption & Expenditure

**Q.** Price of rice rises 25%. By what % should family reduce consumption to maintain same expenditure?
**Ans:** Reduction = 25/(100+25) × 100 = 25/125 × 100 = **20%**

**Q.** Price falls 20%. By what % can consumption increase keeping expenditure same?
**Ans:** Increase = 20/(100−20) × 100 = 20/80 × 100 = **25%**

---

## Variety 6 — Salary & Savings

**Q.** Man spends 70% of income. Income = ₹15000. Saves?
**Ans:** Savings = 30% of 15000 = **₹4500**

**Q.** Man spends 80% of income and saves ₹2400. Income?
**Ans:** Income = 2400 × 100/20 = **₹12000**

**Q.** Income rises 20%, expenditure rises 10%. Original income ₹10000, expenditure ₹7000.
New income = 12000, New expenditure = 7700
New savings = 12000 − 7700 = 4300
Old savings = 3000
% increase = 1300/3000 × 100 = **43.33%**

---

## Variety 7 — Population Problems

**Q.** Population = 50000. Grows 4% p.a. Population after 2 years?
**Ans:** 50000 × (1.04)² = 50000 × 1.0816 = **54080**

**Q.** Population increases 10% year 1, decreases 10% year 2. Net?
**Ans:** Net = 10 + (−10) + (10×−10)/100 = **−1% decrease**

---

## Variety 8 — Election & Voting

**Q.** Candidate gets 55% votes and wins by 2400. Total votes?
**Ans:** Winner = 55%, Loser = 45%, margin = 10% = 2400
Total = 2400/0.10 = **24000**

**Q.** Candidate gets 45% votes, loses by 3600. Total valid votes?
**Ans:** Margin = 55%−45% = 10% = 3600 → Total = **36000**

---

## Variety 9 — Marks & Exam Percentage

**Q.** Student scores 450 out of 600. Percentage?
**Ans:** 450/600 × 100 = **75%**

**Q.** Pass mark is 40%. Student gets 200 marks and fails by 20 marks. Maximum marks?
**Ans:** Pass marks = 220. 40% of max = 220 → Max = **550**

**Q.** A gets 30% and fails by 30. B gets 40% and passes by 10. Maximum marks?
**Ans:** Pass mark = A's marks + 30 = 0.3M + 30
Pass mark = B's marks − 10 = 0.4M − 10
0.3M + 30 = 0.4M − 10 → 0.1M = 40 → M = **400**

---

## Variety 10 — Percentage with Fractions (Speed calculation)

**Q.** 1/4 of a number is 25% more than 20. Find number.
**Ans:** 1/4 × N = 20 × 1.25 = 25 → N = **100**

# TOPIC 2: TIME, SPEED & DISTANCE — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic S=D/T

**Q.** Car travels 360 km in 6 hours. Speed?
**Ans:** 360/6 = **60 km/h**

**Q.** At 72 km/h, time to cover 180 km?
**Ans:** 180/72 = **2.5 hours**

---

## Variety 2 — Unit Conversion

**Q.** Convert 90 km/h to m/s.
**Ans:** 90 × 5/18 = **25 m/s**

**Q.** Convert 15 m/s to km/h.
**Ans:** 15 × 18/5 = **54 km/h**

---

## Variety 3 — Average Speed

**Q.** First half distance at 40 km/h, second half at 60 km/h. Average speed?
**Ans:** 2×40×60/(40+60) = 4800/100 = **48 km/h**

**Q.** First half TIME at 40, second half at 60. Average speed?
**Ans:** Simple average = (40+60)/2 = **50 km/h**

**Critical trap:** Half distance → harmonic mean. Half time → arithmetic mean.

---

## Variety 4 — Relative Speed (Same Direction)

**Q.** A at 60 km/h, B at 40 km/h, same direction. A starts 2 hours after B. When does A catch B?
**Ans:** Head start = 2×40 = 80 km
Time to catch = 80/(60−40) = 80/20 = **4 hours after A starts**

---

## Variety 5 — Relative Speed (Opposite Direction)

**Q.** Two persons walk towards each other from cities 300 km apart at 40 and 60 km/h. When do they meet?
**Ans:** Time = 300/(40+60) = **3 hours**
Meeting point: A covers 40×3 = 120 km from his city

---

## Variety 6 — Train Crossing Pole / Person

**Q.** Train 250m long crosses a pole in 10 sec. Speed?
**Ans:** Speed = 250/10 = 25 m/s = 25×18/5 = **90 km/h**

**Q.** Train at 72 km/h = 20 m/s crosses a stationary man in 15 sec. Length?
**Ans:** L = 20×15 = **300m**

---

## Variety 7 — Train Crossing Platform

**Q.** Train 200m crosses 300m platform in 25 sec. Speed?
**Ans:** Speed = (200+300)/25 = 500/25 = 20 m/s = **72 km/h**

---

## Variety 8 — Two Trains Crossing Each Other

**Q.** Trains 180m and 220m, speeds 60 and 40 km/h, opposite directions. Time to cross?
**Ans:** Relative speed = 100 km/h = 250/9 m/s
Total length = 400m
Time = 400 ÷ 250/9 = 400×9/250 = **14.4 sec**

**Q.** Same trains, same direction. Time to cross?
**Ans:** Relative speed = 20 km/h = 50/9 m/s
Time = 400 ÷ 50/9 = **72 sec**

---

## Variety 9 — Journey with Stoppages

**Q.** Bus covers 240 km in 6 hours including stoppages. Without stoppages speed is 60 km/h. Stoppage per hour?
**Ans:** With stoppage speed = 240/6 = 40 km/h
Speed loss = 20 km/h
Stoppage = 20/60 × 60 = **20 min per hour**

---

## Variety 10 — Time Saved / Lost

**Q.** Walking at 3/4 of usual speed, person is 30 min late. Usual time?
**Ans:** Speed 3/4 → time 4/3. Extra = 1/3 of usual = 30 → Usual = **90 min**

**Q.** If speed increases by 25%, time decreases by?
**Ans:** Decrease = 25/(100+25) × 100 = 20%

---

## Variety 11 — Two-Part Journey

**Q.** Total 100 km. First 60 km at 60 km/h, rest at 40 km/h. Total time?
**Ans:** 60/60 + 40/40 = 1 + 1 = **2 hours**
Average speed = 100/2 = **50 km/h**

---

## Variety 12 — Meeting After Start (same point, opposite)

**Q.** Circular track 1200m. A at 60m/min, B at 40m/min, same direction. When do they meet?
**Ans:** Relative speed = 20m/min → Time = 1200/20 = **60 min**

**Q.** Opposite direction → Time = 1200/100 = **12 min**

# TOPIC 3: TIME & WORK — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic Work Rate

**Q.** A finishes in 12 days, B in 18 days. Together?
**Ans:** LCM = 36. A=3/day, B=2/day. Together=5/day. Days = 36/5 = **7.2 days**

---

## Variety 2 — One Person Leaves Midway

**Q.** A and B together start. After 4 days A leaves. B alone takes 6 more days. B alone can do in?
**Ans:** Let total work = W
Together rate = 1/A + 1/B
Work in 4 days together + 6 days B = W
Need another equation. If A alone = 10 days:
LCM = 10×B/(10+B)... 

Standard version: A does in 20 days, B in 30 days. Work together 6 days, then A leaves. B alone to finish?
LCM = 60. A=3, B=2. Together 6 days = 30 units. Remaining = 30. B alone = 30/2 = **15 days**

---

## Variety 3 — Work and Wages

**Q.** A, B, C do work in 6, 8, 12 days. Total wage ₹2600. Each works full time together. B's wage?
**Ans:** LCM=24. A=4, B=3, C=2. Ratio=4:3:2. Total=9 parts.
B's wage = 2600 × 3/9 = **₹866.67**

**Q.** A works 5 days, B works 7 days. Wage ratio?
**Ans:** If same efficiency → ratio = **5:7**

---

## Variety 4 — Efficiency Comparison (Men/Women/Children)

**Q.** 3 men = 4 women = 6 children in work rate. 6 men + 8 women + 9 children together finish in 20 days. Find days for 1 man alone.
**Ans:** Let child = 1 unit/day. Woman = 6/4 = 1.5, Man = 6/3 = 2
6 men + 8 women + 9 children = 12 + 12 + 9 = 33 units/day
Total work = 33×20 = 660
1 man = 2 units/day → Days = 660/2 = **330 days**

---

## Variety 5 — Man-Days Concept

**Q.** 10 men do work in 15 days. After 5 days, 5 men leave. How many more days?
**Ans:** Total work = 150 man-days
Done in 5 days = 50 man-days. Remaining = 100.
Remaining men = 5. Days = 100/5 = **20 more days**

---

## Variety 6 — Pipes & Cistern (Inlet + Outlet)

**Q.** Inlet fills in 12 hours, outlet empties in 18 hours. Both open. Fill time?
**Ans:** Net = 1/12 − 1/18 = 3/36 − 2/36 = 1/36. Time = **36 hours**

**Q.** Tank fills in 6 hours but leak empties it in 12 hours. With leak, fill time?
**Ans:** Net = 1/6 − 1/12 = 1/12. Time = **12 hours**

---

## Variety 7 — Pipe Opened/Closed at Different Times

**Q.** Inlet A fills in 10 hrs, B fills in 15 hrs. A alone for 3 hrs, then B opens. Total time?
**Ans:** LCM=30. A=3/hr, B=2/hr.
A alone 3 hrs = 9 units. Remaining = 21.
A+B = 5/hr. Time = 21/5 = 4.2 hrs.
Total = 3 + 4.2 = **7.2 hrs**

---

## Variety 8 — Three Workers, Find One

**Q.** A+B = 10 days, B+C = 12 days, A+C = 15 days. Each alone?
**Ans:** 2(A+B+C) = 1/10+1/12+1/15 = 6/60+5/60+4/60 = 15/60 = 1/4
A+B+C = 1/8 per day

A alone = 1/8 − 1/12 = 3/24−2/24 = 1/24 → **24 days**
B alone = 1/8 − 1/15 = 15/120−8/120 = 7/120 → **120/7 days**
C alone = 1/8 − 1/10 = 5/40−4/40 = 1/40 → **40 days**

---

## Variety 9 — Fraction of Work

**Q.** A can do 1/3 of work in 5 days. Total time for A?
**Ans:** 1/3 → 5 days → Full = **15 days**

**Q.** A does 2/5 of work in 6 days. B does rest in 8 days. Who is faster?
**Ans:** A's rate = (2/5)/6 = 2/30 = 1/15 per day (full work in 15 days)
B does 3/5 in 8 days → rate = (3/5)/8 = 3/40 per day (full work in 40/3 = 13.3 days)
**A is faster**

---

## Variety 10 — Group Work Variations

**Q.** 12 men complete work in 18 days. After 6 days 4 men leave. Days to finish remaining?
**Ans:** Total = 216 man-days. Done = 72. Remaining = 144.
Men left = 8. Days = 144/8 = **18 more days**

# TOPIC 4: PROFIT & LOSS — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic P&L

**Q.** CP=₹400, SP=₹480. Profit%?
**Ans:** 80/400 × 100 = **20%**

**Q.** CP=₹600, Loss=10%. SP?
**Ans:** SP = 600 × 90/100 = **₹540**

---

## Variety 2 — Find CP from SP and Profit%

**Q.** SP=₹660, Profit=10%. CP?
**Ans:** CP = 660 × 100/110 = **₹600**

**Q.** SP=₹720, Loss=10%. CP?
**Ans:** CP = 720 × 100/90 = **₹800**

---

## Variety 3 — Markup & Discount

**Q.** MP=₹800, Discount=15%. SP?
**Ans:** SP = 800 × 85/100 = **₹680**

**Q.** CP=₹500, Mark 40% above, give 20% discount. Profit%?
**Ans:** MP = 700. SP = 700×0.8 = 560. Profit = 60/500 × 100 = **12%**

---

## Variety 4 — Successive Discounts

**Q.** Two successive discounts of 20% and 10% on ₹1000. Final price?
**Ans:** After 20% → 800. After 10% → **₹720**

**Q.** Single equivalent discount for 20% and 10%?
**Ans:** Net = 20+10 − (20×10)/100 = 30−2 = **28%**

---

## Variety 5 — Two Articles Same SP, One Profit One Loss

**Q.** Two articles sold at ₹990 each. One at 10% profit, one at 10% loss. Net result?
**Ans:** Always a loss = (10)²/100 = **1% loss**
CP₁ = 990×100/110 = 900, CP₂ = 990×100/90 = 1100
Total CP = 2000, Total SP = 1980. Loss = ₹20.

---

## Variety 6 — Dishonest Dealer (False Weight)

**Q.** Dealer uses 900g instead of 1kg, sells at CP. Profit%?
**Ans:** (1000−900)/900 × 100 = **11.11%**

**Q.** Dealer uses 800g, marks 20% above CP, gives 10% discount. Profit%?
**Ans:** Effective CP = 800/1000 × 100 = ₹80 (for 800g labeled as 1kg)
MP = 100×1.2 = 120. SP = 120×0.9 = 108.
Profit = (108−80)/80 × 100 = **35%**

---

## Variety 7 — Profit on Multiple Items

**Q.** Bought 12 oranges for ₹60, sold 10 for ₹60. Profit%?
**Ans:** CP per orange = 5, SP per orange = 6. Profit = 1/5 × 100 = **20%**

---

## Variety 8 — Cost Price of Multiple Articles

**Q.** CP of 15 articles = SP of 12 articles. Profit%?
**Ans:** Let CP each = 1. Total CP = 15, SP = 15 (since 15 CP = 12 SP → SP each = 15/12)
Profit% = (15/12 − 1) × 100 = (3/12) × 100 = **25%**

---

## Variety 9 — Reverse Markup (Find MP)

**Q.** After 20% discount, SP = ₹720. MP?
**Ans:** MP = 720 × 100/80 = **₹900**

**Q.** After two discounts of 10% and 20%, SP = ₹720. MP?
**Ans:** Effective single discount = 28%. MP = 720 × 100/72 = **₹1000**

---

## Variety 10 — Combined Profit/Loss Percentage

**Q.** Trader sells 1/3 of goods at 10% loss, 2/3 at 15% profit. Overall?
**Ans:** Net = 1/3×(−10) + 2/3×(15) = −10/3 + 10 = −3.33 + 10 = **+6.67% profit**

# TOPIC 5: SIMPLE & COMPOUND INTEREST — ALL VARIETIES

---

## Variety 1 — Basic SI

**Q.** P=₹5000, R=8%, T=3 years. SI?
**Ans:** SI = 5000×8×3/100 = **₹1200**

**Q.** P=₹8000, Amount=₹9400, T=3.5 years. Rate?
**Ans:** SI = 1400. R = 1400×100/(8000×3.5) = **5%**

---

## Variety 2 — Find Principal

**Q.** SI = ₹900, R=6%, T=5 years. Principal?
**Ans:** P = 900×100/(6×5) = **₹3000**

---

## Variety 3 — Find Time

**Q.** P=₹2000, R=5%, SI=₹500. Time?
**Ans:** T = 500×100/(2000×5) = **5 years**

---

## Variety 4 — Amount to Principal (Reverse)

**Q.** Amount = ₹4800 in 4 years at 8% SI. Principal?
**Ans:** P = 100×A/(100+RT) = 100×4800/(100+32) = 480000/132 = **₹3636.36**

---

## Variety 5 — Two Schemes (Split Investment)

**Q.** ₹6000 split at 5% and 8% SI. Total interest in 2 years = ₹780. Amount at 8%?
**Ans:** x at 8%, (6000−x) at 5%
0.16x + 0.10(6000−x) = 780
0.16x + 600 − 0.10x = 780
0.06x = 180 → x = **₹3000**

---

## Variety 6 — SI Equals Principal

**Q.** At what rate does SI equal principal in 10 years?
**Ans:** P×R×10/100 = P → R = **10%**

---

## Variety 7 — Basic CI (Annual)

**Q.** P=₹10000, R=10%, T=2 years CI. Amount?
**Ans:** A = 10000 × (1.1)² = 10000 × 1.21 = **₹12100**
CI = ₹2100

---

## Variety 8 — CI vs SI Difference

**Q.** Difference between CI and SI on ₹5000 at 8% for 2 years?
**Ans:** Difference = P(R/100)² = 5000 × (0.08)² = 5000 × 0.0064 = **₹32**

**Q.** Difference for 3 years at 10% on ₹10000?
**Ans:** = P(R/100)²(3 + R/100) = 10000 × 0.01 × 3.1 = **₹310**

---

## Variety 9 — Half-Yearly / Quarterly CI

**Q.** ₹8000 at 10% p.a. compounded half-yearly for 1 year. Amount?
**Ans:** Rate = 5%, Time = 2 (half-years)
A = 8000 × (1.05)² = 8000 × 1.1025 = **₹8820**

**Q.** ₹10000 at 8% quarterly for 1 year. Amount?
**Ans:** Rate = 2%, Time = 4
A = 10000 × (1.02)⁴ = 10000 × 1.0824 = **₹10824**

---

## Variety 10 — Find Rate from CI Data

**Q.** Sum becomes ₹4800 in 2 years and ₹5184 in 3 years at CI. Rate?
**Ans:** r = 5184/4800 − 1 = 0.08 = **8%**

---

## Variety 11 — Doubling Time

**Q.** At 12.5% SI, when does money double?
**Ans:** P×12.5×T/100 = P → T = **8 years**

**Q.** At 10% CI, when does money double approximately?
**Ans:** Rule of 72: 72/10 = **7.2 years**

---

## Variety 12 — Population / Depreciation

**Q.** Machine worth ₹50000, depreciates 10% p.a. Value after 3 years?
**Ans:** 50000 × (0.9)³ = 50000 × 0.729 = **₹36450**

**Q.** Population 200000, grows 5% p.a. After 2 years?
**Ans:** 200000 × (1.05)² = 200000 × 1.1025 = **220500**

---

## Variety 13 — Installment Problems

**Q.** ₹2000 borrowed at 10% CI. Equal installments for 2 years. Installment amount?
**Ans:** Let installment = x
2000(1.1)² = x(1.1) + x
2420 = 1.1x + x = 2.1x
x = 2420/2.1 = **₹1152.38**

---

## Variety 14 — Variable Rate SI

**Q.** ₹5000 at 4% for 2 years, then 6% for 3 years, then 8% for 1 year. Total SI?
**Ans:** SI₁ = 5000×4×2/100 = 400
SI₂ = 5000×6×3/100 = 900
SI₃ = 5000×8×1/100 = 400
Total = **₹1700**
